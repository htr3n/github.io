About

Huy Tran (aka htr3n) earned his doctorate degree (2005-2009) and started working as software design researcher at at Vienna University of Technology (2009-2011), then University of Vienna (2011-2017) in Austria. Before that, Huy was a lecturer at HCM City University of Technology, Vietnam (2002-2005).

Since starting his research, he had taken part in several European and national R&D projects such as WWTF CONTAINER, EU FP7 INDENICA, EU FP7 COMPAS, and SemBiz. His research interests are including software architecture, distributed systems, SOAs, event-driven architectures, model-driven engineering, and application of formal methods.

Huy has also extensively involved in software development in Java, PHP, JavaScript, Python, Go, and shell scripting. Many of his research outcomes have been transformed to software systems. He developed a view-based modeling framework (VbMF) using Eclipse/EMF/Xtext/Xtend that can be used to design process-driven SOAs and autoamtically generate code and configurations in BPMN/BPEL/WSDL/XML/Java. VbMF was used, in collaboration with some European industrial partners, to model and generate a complex loan approval process-driven system.

Similar ideas of view-based modeling have been developed for designing and generating code and configurations for virtual service platforms running on top of Service-Component Architectures (SCA) and relevant remote communication technologies such as JMS, SOAP/REST. The modeling tool suite was used to design and implement a large-scale platform integration solution that incorporating parts of Yard Management Systems (RoR, Cloud-based), Warehousing Management Systems (.NET, C++), and Realtime Monitoring Systems (Java) from European industrial partners.

Huy’s specialities:

  • Software architecture analysis and design
  • Service-Oriented Architectures and Process-Driven SOAs (BPMN, BPEL)
  • Model-Driven Software Development
  • Event-based systems
  • Domain-specific languages (DSLs)
  • Eclipse Modeling Framework, Xpand/Xtend, and Xtext
  • Web and RESTful services, Spring, Apache CXF, Service Component Architecture
  • Java, PHP/Laravel, Python/Django, JavaScript/Node.js, Go, Shell Scripting
