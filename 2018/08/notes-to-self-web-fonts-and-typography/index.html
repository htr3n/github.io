Web Fonts

Some of my selection criteria

  • Not too narrow: a too narrow font creates too much density and often makes my eyes quickly tired (e.g. Roboto Condensed, Oswald, Open Sans Condensed, PT Sans Narrow, Fjalla One)
  • Not too wide: I don’t feel comfortable reading a sentence looked like being stretched (e.g. Montserrat, Libre Franklin)

Sans-serif fonts

  • IBM Plex Sans and PT Sans look modern and truly impressive. These are among my favourite fonts. Apple default system font for macOS and iOS San Francisco also generate modern and thin, round text shapes.

  • Noto Sans, Lato, and Karla are quite alike with reasonably thick near-square letter shapes. Fira Sans produces character shapes a tad bolder and tighter than Karla.

  • Some other nice fonts like Raleway renders thin and wide text that might look very nice at larger scales rather than typical body font size. Open Sans](https://fonts.google.com/specimen/Open+Sans) looks nice but a bit wide/stretched. Roboto produces round shape, moderate thickness. Nunito renders elegant and thin sans-serif text. Both Alegreya Sans and Source Sans Pro look nice and remind me of Myriad Pro.

Serif fonts

  • Eczar - nice and very impressive serif font

  • Merriweather - at the same font size, it looks a bit bolder and larger than other serif fonts

  • Neuton - looks smaller for the same font size

  • PT Serif - nice looking

  • Slabo 27px – looks modern, narrow than others

  • Lora and Source Serif Pro render not bad and quite modern

  • Cormorant - looks thin and similar to Garamond. Cardo and Crimson Text look similar to the oldie Times and Times New Roman.

Monospace

  • Roboto Mono

  • Inconsolata

  • Source Code Pro
